New to stocks? How to read this page
Signal (stance)The tool's overall read of each stock's chart. BUY = setup looks favourable. HOLD = no clear edge, do nothing. SELL = weak chart, avoid or exit.
Buy zone (entry)The price range where buying has the best balance of risk and reward. If the price has already run above the zone, don't chase it.
Stop-lossYour safety exit. If the price falls to this level, the idea has failed — sell and accept a small loss. Never trade without one.
TargetsLevels where the tool suggests booking profit — sell part at Target 1, the rest at Target 2.
Reward : riskPotential gain vs. potential loss. 1:2 means risking ₹1 to potentially make ₹2. The tool rejects anything below 1:2.
ConfidenceHow much chart evidence supports the signal (0–100). It is not a probability of profit — 60 means "decent evidence", never "sure thing".
Golden rules for beginners: risk no more than 1% of your money on any single idea · always place the stop-loss the moment you buy · start tiny or paper-trade first · signals are computed from price history and can simply be wrong.
